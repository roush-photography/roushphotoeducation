"How do I, as a photographer, increase my skill level and get better ... better than
I was yesterday, or last week, or last year?"

---------------------------------------------------------------------

Over the past few years I've been ask by many individuals this very question:

Many of us remain within our comfort zone, even when ask to move out of it. The road to improvement gets tougher
as we get better.
Consider this example below an observation.

Let's say your skill level ranks you at a 60 (1 being the lowest, and 100 being the highest).
To get from 0 to 30 whne you started photography is pretty easy for most, and takes just a short time.
Going from 30 to 45 in ability takes more time than your first segment of learning. Getting from 45 to 50
is ridiculously hard, and takes a very long time comparatively. Moving from 50-60 requires a lot of studying,
many workshops, workgroups, and self-motivated progress. Moving forward from this point, to some, is seemingly impossible.
It's VERY difficult to continue the learning process when you are at a higher skill level plateau. Many of you have discovered this already..

That's where this workshop will assist you. The discussions we will have will be about moving forward and UP the skill level
ladder from where your skill level stands right now. This is about thinking differently, applying our knowledge differently,
and extending our thought processes to a new undiscovered areas in our creative minds.

This workshop will be different in that it will be a big think-tank type discussion group. This isn't just about the numbers, the lenses,
the cameras, and all the other struggles we've faced. This is about finally making sense out of ALL of this in our OWN minds.
Reaching deeper, finding new creative forces, and pushing our own imaginations firther will be one of the many results of this program.

You will need to bring your cameras, lenses, and a tripod.
Also, be prepared to be challenged personally with thought-provoking questions that might be tough to answer.
Be ready to question the reasons you do the things you do. And, be ready to critique your own photographic
behavior like you have never critiqued it before.

This program will open your mind to new possibilities, new thoughts, and new creative energy.
