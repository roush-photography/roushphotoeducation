Starting Your Own Photography Business

One of the challenges every photographer faces is that moment when your "hobbyist" photographer status
wants to change to "professional" photographer status. Oddly, this seems to happen somewhat automatically
when your skill levels reach a point of maturity, and not when you are necesarily ready.

This manual will take you through the steps and structures of business that you need to get started correctly.
It covers forming a business plan, some ideas on licenses and taxes, marketing and advertising effectively,
and defining your busness. These are just a few of the major topics covered. Logos, promotional materials,
what to charge, and selling are also covered. The manual is 25 pages of serious photographic business info.

This manual was originally written to accompany a course I offer called "How to Start of Photo Business",
and it still does. I've not sold the book separately until now.

I've had so many requests for help and guidance over this past year that I've decided to offer this manual
directly to photographers. If you're invited to take advantage of this special private offer then I have already
evaluated you to some degree and feel you are ready to take this step.

Purchasing this book will educate you on the tools you need and keep you on the right path over the next
few months as you push forward in starting a business. As many of you have found, simply developing a
"Facebook" page for your photo business has really no value in the long run. There are certaing things you need
to do in order to command web presence and credentials; things you must show to the public that elevates
you above the Facebook crowd. We all want to be "this" photographer, and this book will help you reach that goal.

With this purchase I'm including a couple of hours of mentoring time with me to discuss your
Photo Business plans, ideas, and goals
